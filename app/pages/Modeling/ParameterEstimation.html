    Description

    [a] In the referenced article, the original rate constant is 0.23 nM/min for an operator content of 2.08 nM. This yields the rate constant $\ce{k_\text{MR} = 0.11}$ min-1

    [b] The transcription rate is on average 50 nucleotides per second in E. coli [7]. The NahR transcript has approximately 900 nucleotides which yields $\ce{k_\text{MN}=3.33}$ min-1. The GFP10-linker-ER-αLBD-linker-GFP11 transcript has approximately 1800 nucleotides which yields $\ce{k_\text{ME}=1.67}$ min-1. The GFP1-9 transcript has approximately 600 nucleotides which yields $\ce{k_\text{MG} = 5}$ min-1.

    [c] The translation rate is on average 15 amino acids per second in E. coli [7]. NahR has approximately 300 amino acids which yields $\ce{k_\text{MN}=3}$ min-1. GFP10-linker-ER-αLBD-linker-GFP11 has approximately 600 amino acids which yields $\ce{k_\text{ME}=1.5}$ min-1. The GFP1-9 transcript has approximately 200 amino acids which yields $\ce{k_\text{MG}=4.5}$ min-1.

    [d] Antag irreversibel. Man förväntar sig knappast att ett vanligt GFP ska dissociera i två fragment. Och om det gör det så är det genom nedbrytning.

    [e] Nedbrytningshastigheter väljs så att modellen blir rimlig. Runt sex timmar är vanligt för proteiner? [källa]

    [f] The constant was estimated from the permability $p$ using the relation $k = p \frac{A}{V}$ where $A$ is the surface area and $V$ the cell volume. We assume that the cell is spherical with volume $\ce{V=8 \cdot 10^{-16}}$ L, yielding the constant $\ce{k_\text{dS}=0.281}$ min-1 from $\ce{p=9 \cdot 10^{-8}}$ cm s-1 [3].

    [g] The constant was estimated from the diffusion constant $D$ using the relation $k = \frac{D}{A}$ where $A$ is the surface area. We assume that the cell is spherical with volume $\ce{V=8\cdot 10^{-16}}$ L, yielding $\ce{k_\text{dE}=1.44}$ min-1 from $\ce{D=10^{7}}$ Å2/s. [4]

    [h] Half lifes for common fluorescent proteins has been shown to range over one to two hours [7]. We choose a half-life of 120 min, yielding the rate constant $\lambda = \log(2)/120$ min-1.





    The transcription rate is on average 50 nucleotides per second (stochastic processes in cell biology, cell biology by numbers).
    The translation rate is on average 15 amino acids per second (Cell Biology By numbers).
    The average lifetime of mRNA is assumed to be 5 min (Cell biology by numbers).
    transcription: 50 nucleotides/sec = 3000 nucleotides/min translation: 15 amino acids/sec = 900 amino acids/mi n
    NahR: 300 amino acids -> 900 nucleotides -> mRNA: 0.3 min^-1. protein: 0.33 min^-1
    GFP10-link-ERa-link-GFP11: 606 amino acids -> 1818 nucleotides -> mRNA 0.606 min^-1 -> 0.673 min^-1
    Maturation time sfGFP 6min ref
    Half-life sgGFP: 28 min - 119 min for R-MCherry GFP. We assume a half-life of 120 min for our sfGFP. link.
    Half lifes for common fluorescent proteins has been shown to range over one to two hours [bionumbers]. We take a value of 120 minutes. -> $\lambda_\text{G} = \log{(2)}/120 \text{min}^{-1}$
    For the estrogen receptor alpha, the half-life varies from 2 to 24 hours in human (link). We assume a half-life of 6 hours.
    The folding times for the proteins are estimated as directly proportional to the sequence length via the relation $N^{1/2} = \log(\tau)$, where N is the chain length and $\tau$ is the folding time in microseconds. [källa].
    For membrane diffusion rates estimated from the permeability $p$, we estimate the rate constants as $\text{k} = p\frac{A}{V}$.
    The NahR rate constants were obtained by fitting our system of equations to induction ratios taken from literature [link], assuming an expression of 100 nM at base level and a steady state after 4 hours.
